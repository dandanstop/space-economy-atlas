# Latest Signals Pipeline Source Families

Use this file to guide signal collection. The goal is not to collect everything. The goal is to collect recent items that are educationally meaningful and relevant to the atlas.

## 1. National And Intergovernmental Agencies

Use for:

- stable program updates
- mission announcements
- infrastructure changes
- policy direction
- station, launch, satellite, and exploration milestones

Core families:

- NASA
- ESA
- JAXA
- ISRO
- CAS and related official institutes
- China Manned Space official channels when accessible
- national space science centers and observatories

## 2. Mission Operators And Applied Research Centers

Use for:

- engineering signals
- payload and mission operations
- applied research milestones
- infrastructure readiness

Examples:

- JPL
- Johns Hopkins APL
- national observatories
- space science centers

## 3. University Labs And Research Institutes

Use for:

- future capability signals
- prototypes
- experimental systems
- robotics
- orbital compute
- materials
- communications
- sensing

Regional priority:

- United States
- Europe
- Japan
- India
- China
- Taiwan

Examples:

- MIT Space Systems Laboratory
- UC Berkeley Space Sciences Laboratory
- LASP
- Beihang University
- Harbin Institute of Technology
- National Astronomical Observatories, CAS

## 4. Official Company Sources

Use for:

- launches
- contracts
- new services
- constellation updates
- station development
- terminals and connectivity
- in-orbit services
- orbital compute infrastructure

Rules:

- official newsroom, mission, or technical page preferred
- ignore generic promotional posts unless they contain concrete operational updates

## 5. Papers And Preprints

Use sparingly for signals.

Good fits:

- important preprints tied to emerging orbital infrastructure
- novel on-orbit compute, robotics, autonomy, servicing, or sustainability work
- papers with clear relevance to the site's future-signals angle

Do not allow papers to dominate the queue unless the pass is explicitly research-focused.
