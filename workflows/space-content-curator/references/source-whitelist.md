# Space Content Curator Source Whitelist

Use this file to guide source selection for `space-content-curator`. These are preferred primary-source families for educational space-economy content.

## 1. National And Intergovernmental Agencies

Use for:

- `Why it matters`
- `Government programs`
- `Future signals`
- stable source links

Preferred families:

- NASA
- ESA
- JAXA
- ISRO
- CNSA-adjacent official mission and institute pages when publicly accessible
- WEF when the topic is ecosystem framing rather than technical mission detail

## 2. Mission Operators And Applied Research Centers

Use for:

- `How it works`
- `Research notes`
- deeper engineering context

Preferred families:

- JPL
- Johns Hopkins APL
- mission operation centers with official technical pages
- national observatories and space science centers

## 3. University Labs And Research Institutes

Use for:

- `How it works`
- `Research notes`
- `Future signals`
- technical explainers and student-to-industry pipeline context

### United States

- MIT Space Systems Laboratory
- UC Berkeley Space Sciences Laboratory
- LASP at University of Colorado Boulder
- Caltech-affiliated space research groups
- university labs with direct mission hardware, operations, robotics, or orbital systems work

### Europe

- university labs tied to ESA missions or orbital systems work
- applied aerospace programs with mission pages, lab publications, and public explainers

### Japan

- ISAS research pages
- University of Tokyo affiliated deep-space or spacecraft research groups
- other JAXA-linked academic labs

### India

- IISc aerospace and space-research programs
- IIT labs directly tied to space systems, remote sensing, propulsion, autonomy, or satellite engineering
- academic centers connected to ISRO ecosystem work

### China

Core whitelist:

- Chinese Academy of Sciences
- National Astronomical Observatories, CAS
- National Space Science Center, CAS
- Beihang University
- Harbin Institute of Technology
- Northwestern Polytechnical University

Use for:

- space science
- deep-space exploration
- astronomy
- aerospace engineering
- manufacturing, controls, materials, and talent pipeline context

Guidance:

- Prefer official English pages
- Prefer institute, observatory, lab, or school pages over generic campus news
- If only Chinese pages are available, keep them in `Sources` or `editor notes` unless an editor approves homepage use

### Taiwan

- National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University space-related labs
- National Central University remote sensing and space science programs
- National Tsing Hua University relevant aerospace or satellite research groups
- other official university centers with public technical material

## 4. Companies To Explore

Use for:

- `Companies to explore`
- ecosystem maps
- value-chain examples

Rules:

- official website only
- identify the company role, such as launch, satellite manufacturing, in-space services, remote sensing, terminals, ground systems, or downstream software
- do not frame inclusion as endorsement

Suggested split:

- public companies
- private companies

## 5. Papers And Preprints

Use for:

- `Research notes`
- `Future signals`
- supporting detail behind emerging concepts

Preferred families:

- arXiv
- peer-reviewed journals
- conference proceedings tied to space engineering, satellite systems, robotics, astronomy, or orbital infrastructure

Rules:

- do not let papers dominate the homepage narrative
- summarize the practical meaning in plain language
- treat early-stage claims as emerging, not established

## 6. Fit By Content Slot

### Summary

Best inputs:

- agency explainers
- mission overview pages
- official program pages

### Why It Matters

Best inputs:

- agencies
- government programs
- public-interest research centers
- international policy and infrastructure reports

### How It Works

Best inputs:

- university labs
- mission operators
- research centers
- technical company explainers

### Government Programs

Best inputs:

- official agencies
- ministry-backed space programs
- national science institutes

Target:

- include 3 or more meaningful links when the chapter warrants it

### Research Notes

Best inputs:

- labs
- observatories
- mission research teams
- paper and publication pages

### Companies To Explore

Best inputs:

- official company websites
- companies with a real connection to the chapter topic

### Future Signals

Best inputs:

- mission demos
- new lab programs
- new infrastructure
- in-orbit compute
- on-orbit servicing
- manufacturing breakthroughs
- new policy or procurement signals
